I started on the web before it was an industry. The early work was craft in the literal sense — cobbling together systems from whatever was available, learning by shipping, understanding technology from the inside out. That foundation runs through everything I do now: I think about organisations the way I once thought about software systems, looking for the structures that produce the outcomes I'm seeing.

By 2010, I'd worked across software delivery, architecture, programme management, and a first encounter with agile. The question I couldn't stop asking was why some teams thrived in complexity and others didn't.
