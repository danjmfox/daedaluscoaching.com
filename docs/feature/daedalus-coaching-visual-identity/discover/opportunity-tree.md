## Desired Outcome

**For prospective clients (all three tiers)**: arrive at the site and within 5 seconds receive a coherent signal that this is a rigorous, legitimate, differentiated coaching practice — without needing to read a word of copy.

**For the owner**: commit to a visual direction that does not need to be revised. Ship it. Not revert it. The test of success is the absence of a 5th experiment.

**For DISCUSS wave**: receive a brief specific enough to make committed design token decisions — typography, colour, spacing — without returning to DISCOVER for clarification.

---

## The Central Tension

All opportunities below are shaped by one unresolved tension the 4 failures made visible:

> The practice must signal rigour (ICF accreditation, B-Corp, senior organisational clients) _and_ warmth (coaching relationship, human development, individual leaders). Every previous visual direction resolved this tension by choosing one pole. The opportunity is to hold both without visual compromise.

This tension is not unique to Daedalus Coaching — it is the fundamental challenge of any practice operating at the intersection of systems thinking and human transformation. The visual identity that holds it well will be differentiated by default.

## The Brief Resolution Framework

OPP-V1 is the highest-priority opportunity and the blocker for all others. DISCUSS cannot begin until this brief exists. The brief must answer five questions:

**Q1 — What must the visual identity communicate?** (In order of priority)

Based on evidence from prior discovery and failure pattern analysis:

1. This practitioner is credible and accredited (not a generic coach) — serves all three tiers
2. This practice operates at the rigorous, senior end of the market — primarily serves organisation/team tier
3. The practitioner is a real person with a distinct philosophy, not a brand archetype — serves individual tier
4. The practice is aligned with values the client recognises (B-Corp, 1%FTP) — differentiator, secondary to credibility

**Q2 — To whom?** (Primary audience for visual identity decisions)

The visual identity must be calibrated for the hardest-to-satisfy evaluator. That is the organisation/team buyer (HR Director, programme sponsor, L&D lead) — they have the highest credibility bar, are least likely to give benefit of the doubt, and are making a vendor selection decision. If the identity works for them, it will work for individual leaders (who arrive with more trust and less formality).

**Q3 — What must it visually NOT be?** (From failure pattern)

- Not metaphor-driven (no paper, no boxes, no postit, no skewed elements — decoration without coherence)
- Not corporate-generic (navy + stock photos + "I help leaders unlock their potential")
- Not startup-agile (bold colour pops, sans-serif with attitude, sprint-board aesthetic)
- Not "trying to look sophisticated" (the result of over-designed coaching sites — the shadcn signal inverted)

**Q4 — What must it hold in tension?** (The central design problem)

Rigour (ICF, B-Corp, senior clients, evidence-based practice) _and_ warmth (coaching relationship, human development, the practitioner's individual voice). This tension cannot be resolved by choosing one pole. It must be held.

**Q5 — What does "settled" look like?** (The done criterion)

The owner reviews the committed direction after 2 weeks of use and does not want to change it. The comparables exercise (Phase 3) must include at least 3 examples where the owner says "that direction works" — not just "I like it" but "I would not want to change it."

## Sector Comparables Shortlist (for Phase 3)

These are starting-point reference categories. The comparables exercise must include:

**Correct comparables — practitioners who hold rigour/warmth well:**

- Senior executive coaches with ICF PCC/MCC accreditation and a distinct written voice
- Organisational development consultancies with B-Corp certification
- Facilitation practices at the intersection of systems thinking and human transformation

**Anti-comparables — what Daedalus must not look like:**

- Generic ICF coach directories (corporate blue, stock photography, "certified coach" headlines)
- Agile consultancy websites (process diagrams, sprint metaphors, navy/orange palette)
- Design-forward portfolio sites (visually impressive, credibility-absent)

**Adjacent field comparables — practices that hold the tension well in different domains:**

- Medical practitioners who communicate both clinical rigour and patient-centred warmth
- Academic-practitioner consultants (research credibility + accessible communication)
- Values-led professional services firms (law or accountancy with B-Corp certification)
