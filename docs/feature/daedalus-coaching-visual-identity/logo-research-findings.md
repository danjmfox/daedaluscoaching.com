## The Mark

A dual-colour split bird in **deep teal** (left: body + left wing, reading as a **D**) and **coral/orange** (right: wing only, reading as a **C**), with layered feather detail lines in cream/off-white negative space. Together the two shapes form a symmetrical spread-wing bird. The D+C letterforms embed the Daedalus Coaching initials.

## What Is Clear

**The mark is not occupied territory.** No globally prominent brand in coaching, professional services, or the Agile certification sector uses a teal+coral dual-colour split bird mark. Bird imagery is present in coaching branding but is not the dominant cliché — arrows, mountains, paths, and lotus flowers are. The mark is differentiating within the space.

**The D+C device is an asset.** It makes the mark a monogram-bird hybrid. This distinguishes it from pure heraldic eagle tradition, reduces the fascist association risk, and gives the mark a functional rationale (identity, not decoration) that aligns with the brief's "no metaphors" spirit at the mark level.

**Teal+coral is a recognised pairing**, not novel but not clichéd in coaching. The palette reads as modern and warm. It is not owned by any competitor or governing body as a primary identity.

## Active Risks Requiring Action

### 1. Trademark search (non-optional before launch)

A professional figurative mark search must be commissioned against:

- **UK IPO** — Nice Class 41 (coaching/training/education) and Class 35 (business consulting)
- **EUIPO** — Same classes, using Vienna Classification 3.7 (birds in flight) as a starting filter
- **USPTO** — Same classes

Tools: Trademarkia, Corsearch, TMview, or a trademark attorney.

This is the one gap that research could not close. All other risks are manageable by design or copy decisions. This one requires external professional input.

### 2. Dove silhouette — cold-read test

Render the logo in greyscale and show it to 5–10 people in the target audience (senior professionals, no context given). Ask what they see. If a majority say "dove" and associate peace/charity/NGO, the silhouette needs refinement:

- More angular posture
- Heavier wing weight
- Less rounded beak extension

If the majority read "bird in flight" or "wings" without a dove association, the silhouette is clear.

### 3. Bird head posture — single design check

Confirm the bird's head is **forward-facing or slightly tilted**, not turned sharply over one shoulder. The over-the-shoulder profile is the single most culturally loaded posture in eagle/Reichsadler imagery. This is a low-effort check with meaningful risk mitigation.

### 4. ICF colour verification

Download the ICF brand style guide from coachingfederation.org and extract their teal and coral HEX values. Compare against `#2a616a` and `#ff795a`. The risk is downgraded to Medium but worth a direct HEX comparison before committing to the palette publicly.

## Narrative Requirement

The Daedalus/Icarus myth is predominantly encoded in public memory as a hubris-and-fall cautionary tale. The Icarus Paradox (Miller, 1990) is an active leadership framework reinforcing this. A soaring bird mark attached to the name "Daedalus" reinforces the wrong character in the story.

**The fix is copy, not mark.** The brand narrative must establish:

> Daedalus was the craftsman-father who designed the wings and survived. He built the labyrinth. He solved problems that couldn't be solved. The coaching practice takes his name because the work is craft — building the thinking structures that allow complex problems to be held clearly.

This framing is achievable and accurate to the myth. It must appear in the About page, bio copy, and any brand introduction — not assumed from the mark alone.

## What Was Ruled Out

- **Religious/spiritual conflict**: Scientology's primary symbol is a cross-with-rays, not a bird. The Christian Holy Spirit dove is white on white — categorical colour distance. No structural overlap.
- **Vale corporation**: Structurally similar two-tone bird device but categorically different industry (mining) and geography (Brazil). Zero trademark conflict in Class 41/35.
- **Phoenix**: Shape convention (flames, tail plumage, upward resurrection posture) is sufficiently distinct from a spread-wing soaring bird. Low risk unless feather lines graduate toward flame tones.
